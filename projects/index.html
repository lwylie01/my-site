The Barnum Test

Does your personality type actually measure anything? A 90-second interactive experiment that builds you a "personalized" profile, asks how accurate it feels — then reveals that everyone gets the identical profile, and that your answers were used for nothing. The cleanest possible lesson in construct validity, disguised as a party trick.

Counted Wrong: Emerging Adults & the Crossroads Study

A deep dive into longitudinal data from the Crossroads Study — following young people through their first contact with the justice system — to ask a question the categories were never built to answer: where does adolescence end and adulthood begin? The legal system draws a hard line at 18; the developmental data draws nothing of the kind. A project about what happens when a measurement boundary gets treated as a biological one.

Applied Research · Aging & the Law

A Right That Exists on Paper: Compassionate Release

Nearly every state and the federal system has a compassionate or medical release law on the books — a legal mechanism to release people who are dying, gravely ill, or simply too old to pose a risk. Almost none of them get used. Drawing on a fifty-state review and the data behind Aging and the Criminal Legal System (APA, 2025), this project measures the distance between a law's presence and its utilization — and asks what an unused statute reveals about how a system measures mercy.

How Old Is Old?

A 60-second experiment: drag a slider to mark the age where someone becomes "old," "a senior," "too old to drive," "too old to start over." Then watch how little anyone agrees — and how the law quietly fixes hard numbers (65, 50, 18) onto a boundary that doesn't exist in nature. Operationalization made personal. It's Counted Wrong at the other end of the lifespan.

Trust Your Gut?

Read a handful of real-looking case files and predict who comes back to court and who doesn't. Then get scored against a dead-simple statistical rule that uses two variables and beats you anyway. The most uncomfortable lesson in applied prediction — that expert intuition reliably loses to a checklist — delivered as a game you'll want to replay.
